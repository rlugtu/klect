# Klect — Caption Styling + Launch Captions & Hashtags

Two parts: (1) exact CapCut settings so your text looks native to TikTok instead of default, and (2) ready-to-post captions + hashtags for each video on both platforms.

---

## PART 1 — CapCut caption styling (native TikTok look)

There are two kinds of text in your ads, and they should look different:
- **Spoken captions** — the auto-generated subtitles of the voiceover/UGC script. Small-ish, bottom third, word-by-word.
- **On-screen headline text** — your big bold lines ("Your bookmarks are where good ideas go to die"). Large, centered, one line at a time.

### A. Spoken captions setup

1. **Generate them:** CapCut → Text → **Auto Captions** → select your audio track → generate. Fix any misheard words.
2. **Font:** a heavy, rounded sans-serif. In CapCut's font list, pick a **bold** option (the default caption fonts like the standard bold sans read as native — avoid thin, serif, or decorative fonts). Bold is non-negotiable; thin text disappears on busy video.
3. **Size:** roughly 8–10% of frame height. Big enough to read at a glance, not so big it eats the screen.
4. **Position:** center-aligned, sitting about **25–30% up from the bottom** — above TikTok's caption/username UI, which covers the bottom ~320px.
5. **Readability (this is what makes or breaks it):**
   - Color: pure white, 100% opacity.
   - **Stroke/outline:** black, ~8–12px. This is the single most important setting — it keeps text legible over any background.
   - Optional: subtle drop shadow, or a semi-transparent rounded background box at ~40% opacity for extra pop.
6. **Word-by-word reveal (the "native" feel):** use the **karaoke / highlight** caption style — words appear as spoken, with the *current* word highlighted in a color. Set the highlight to your **brand accent** (e.g. one of your tag colors). This word-by-word rhythm is what makes TikTok captions feel current rather than like burned-in subtitles.

### B. On-screen headline text (the big lines)

1. **Add as separate Text**, not captions. One line per shot.
2. **Font:** same bold sans family for consistency, but sized much larger — **14–18% of frame height**, centered vertically in the middle 60% of the screen.
3. **Color/stroke:** white with the same black stroke, OR flip it — brand-accent text with white stroke — to make headlines visually distinct from the spoken captions.
4. **Animation IN:** use **"Pop"** or **"Bounce"** (fast, ~0.2–0.3s). Snappy, not slow fades — fades feel sluggish on short-form.
5. **Timing:** the headline should hit **on the beat** and stay for its full 2–4s shot, then hard-cut out with the shot. Don't let text linger past its clip.
6. **Emphasis trick:** on key words (e.g. "**buried**", "**free**"), bump size 20% or switch to the accent color for one word. Draws the eye.

### C. Copy-these-settings recipe

| Setting | Spoken captions | Headline text |
|---|---|---|
| Font weight | Bold sans | Bold sans (larger) |
| Size (% of height) | 8–10% | 14–18% |
| Color | White | White or brand accent |
| Stroke | Black 8–12px | Black 8–12px |
| Position | 25–30% from bottom | Centered, middle 60% |
| Animation | Karaoke word highlight | Pop / Bounce in (~0.25s) |
| Highlight color | Brand accent | — |

### D. Two things to avoid
- **No CapCut robot voice** for the VO — use your own recorded voice or the UGC tool's avatar voice. AI text-to-speech reads as low-effort.
- **Don't over-animate.** One clean animation style throughout. Mixing five effects looks amateur.

## PART 2 — Launch captions & hashtags

### Strategy notes (2026)
- **Fewer, targeted hashtags beat big dumps.** Use 3–5 relevant tags, not 20. Both platforms now lean on caption *keywords* (SEO) as much as hashtags — so write captions with real words your audience searches ("bookmarking app," "save restaurants").
- **TikTok captions:** short, casual, hook-forward. Front-load a line that makes people stop.
- **Instagram captions:** can run slightly longer with a light story or line breaks; end with a soft CTA.
- **Always add a CTA** to install or follow, and put the App Store link in bio (both platforms limit clickable caption links).

### Posting cadence
- Post organically **3–5×/week per platform** to start, one angle per post, rotating captions/hashtags.
- Repost your best organic performers as **paid** (swap trending audio for licensed).
- Watch the first 3 seconds' retention — if a video drops fast there, re-cut the hook and repost rather than abandoning the concept.
